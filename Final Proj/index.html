Dòu Huā

If there was only one dessert Kristie could have for the rest of her life, it would be tofu pudding. She is truly obsessed. Her friends and family are as puzzled about this as you are. Here are some yummy dòu huā spots in Taiwan!
[ taipei ]

白水豆花

Da'an district, Taipei City

Located in Yong Kang Street, Bai-Shui Tofu Pudding is surrounded by dozens of popular shops and restaurants. My friends and I came for dessert one night, after having dinner nearby. The storefront is small, with just a few wooden tables next to the stand where they serve dòu huā. The tofu pudding here is extremely soft, and each delicate bowl is topped with peanut powder and coriander. Although a strange combination at first glance, it’s unexpectedly tasty. You can even add tapioca pearls and peach resin!
[ tainan ]

筱庭杏仁豆腐冰、豆花

Xinhua district, Tainan City

A long-time favorite of my aunt’s, this wonderful dessert spot has been around for many years. The store is slightly hidden at the end of the bustling area of Xin Hua in Tainan city. We came one afternoon for their almond pudding – which is the best I’ve ever had – and saw that they also sold dòu huā. The owner of the shop noticed how excited I was to see that they had tofu pudding, and graciously gifted us a bowl. The tofu pudding we tried here was dòu huā in its most traditional form – a few slices of dòu huā submerged in a sugary syrup.
[ kinmen ]

阿公ㄟ豆花

Kinmen county, Jincheng Township

This past winter break, my family spent a few days exploring Kinmen, one of Taiwan’s beautiful island counties. In our quest for good eats, we came across 阿公ㄟ豆花, located in the historic Mofan Street. Here, you can get a big bowl of dòu huā and add all the toppings your heart desires with no additional charge! The best part about coming here in the winter is the ginger syrup. Soft, sweet and ever-so slightly spicy, this bowl of dòu huā is the perfect pick me up after a long day of exploring.
